About Bangalore Metro

Bangalore Metro Rail, popularly known as Namma Metro, is a modern rapid transit system designed to reduce traffic congestion and improve urban mobility in Bengaluru.

It connects major residential areas, IT hubs, commercial centers, and educational institutions, ensuring faster, safer, and stress-free travel for daily commuters.

Metro Lines in Bengaluru

  • Purple Line – Connects Whitefield (Kadugodi) in the east to Challaghatta in the southwest.
    • Length: 43.45 km.
    • Key areas: Baiyappanahalli, MG Road, Majestic, Mysore Road, Kengeri, ITPL.
  • Green Line – Runs north-south, connecting Madavara in the north-west to Silk Institute in the south-west.
    • Length: 33.46 km.
    • Key areas: Yeshwanthpur, Majestic, Lalbagh, Jayanagara, Banashankari.
  • Yellow LineThe newest operational line, connecting Rashtreeya Vidyalaya (RV) Road to Bommasandra (Electronic City).
    • Length: 19.15 km
    • Key areas: BTM Layout, Jayadeva Hospital (interchange), Central Silk Board (interchange), Electronic City.

Color-coded metro lines simplify route identification and help passengers travel efficiently across the city.

Why Bangalore Metro is Preferred

  • Time-saving compared to road transport
  • Comfortable air-conditioned coaches
  • Fixed schedules & reliable services
  • Affordable fares for daily commuters
  • Eco-friendly public transportation

Conclusion

Namma Metro has transformed urban travel in Bengaluru by providing a safe, efficient, and sustainable public transportation system.

It plays a vital role in reducing traffic congestion and supports the vision of a smart and connected city.
